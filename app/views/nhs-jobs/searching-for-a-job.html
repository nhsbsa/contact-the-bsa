When searching for a job, the search results may differ depending on the search field and filters you've used.  

When searching using 'Job title or skills', our search will prioritise job listings with the key word in the job title, followed by similar results to the search term.

The results will be shown in 'best match' order. This means that the jobs closest to your search results will be shown first.

When searching by an employer's name, it will then display similar employers to give you more choice.

The NHS Jobs search will return any jobs that fully or partially match your search criteria.

Other jobs that only contain some of the words used in your search criteria, will show later in the list. This could be job title or employer name.

This will help if you're looking for a general area of work such as 'management', but will mean that jobs will become less relevant as the list goes on.

You can also use the filters at the side of the page to refine your search results. You can filter by pay grade or band, distance or working arrangements like full-time or part-time.
